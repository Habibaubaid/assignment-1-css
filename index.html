BILL GATES

500 Fifth Avenue North,Seattle, WA 98109,USA info@gatesfoundation.org

Bill Gates is the co-founder of Microsoft and one of the most influential figures in the tech industry. He is also a leading philanthropist through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, focusing on global health and education

EXPERIENCE

Global Philanthropy:

Co-chair of the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, working on global health, poverty reduction, education, and vaccine development.

Climate Innovation:

Founder of Breakthrough Energy, investing in clean energy technologies to fight climate change.

Technology Advisor:

Still provides insights on technology trends, AI, and innovation, though he’s no longer active in Microsoft’s daily operations.

EDUCATION

Early Education:

Attended Lakeside School in Seattle, Washington — a private preparatory school.It was at Lakeside that Gates was first introduced to computers and programming.

College:

Enrolled at Harvard University in 1973. Studied pre-law, mathematics, and computer science courses. Met Steve Ballmer (future Microsoft CEO) at Harvard.



Did Not Graduate:

Gates left Harvard in 1975 to co-found Microsoft with his childhood friend Paul Allen.He later received several honorary degrees from institutions like Harvard (2007), recognizing his contributions to technology and philanthropy.

INTERESTS


Bill Gates has a wide range of interests that reflect his curiosity and desire to make the world a better place. He is deeply interested in technology and innovation, always exploring how new advancements like artificial intelligence, biotechnology, and clean energy can solve global problems. Through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, he focuses on improving global health, education, and poverty reduction, showing his strong commitment to philanthropy. Gates is also passionate about climate change and sustainability, investing in projects that promote renewable energy and reduce carbon emissions. Beyond his professional work, he enjoys reading, learning, and sharing knowledge through his blog, GatesNotes. In his free time, he likes playing bridge, tennis, and spending time with family and friends, reflecting his balance between intellectual pursuits and personal enjoyment.

AWARDS AND CERTIFICATIONS

  • Time Person of the Year (2005) – with Melinda Gates and Bono, for humanitarian work.
  • National Medal of Technology and Innovation (1992) – awarded by the President of the United States.
  • Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire (KBE) (2005) – honorary knighthood from Queen Elizabeth II for his services to global enterprise and charity.
  • Lasker-Bloomberg Public Service Award (2013) – for contributions to improving global health.
  • Padma Bhushan (2015) – one of India’s highest civilian awards, for philanthropic efforts.
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (2016) – the highest civilian award in the U.S., presented by President Barack Obama.
  • Bower Award for Business Leadership (2010) – from the Franklin Institute.
  • Silver Buffalo Award (2010) – from the Boy Scouts of America for service to youth.
  • Hilal-e-Pakistan (2022) – Pakistan’s second-highest civilian award, for efforts in polio eradication.
  • Honorary Doctorates – from several universities worldwide, including Harvard, Cambridge, and Tsinghua University.
